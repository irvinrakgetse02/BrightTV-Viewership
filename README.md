# 🎬 BrightTV Viewership Analytics & Growth Strategy

## 📊 Project Overview
This repository delivers an end-to-end data analytics solution evaluating subscriber behavior, regional market share, and content engagement patterns for **BrightTV**. 

By processing messy operational records into clean, multi-variable data models, this project arms the **Customer Value Management (CVM)** team and executive leadership with the precise, empirical insights needed to scale the platform's active subscription base for the financial year.

---

## 🎯 Business Objectives
* **Scale Subscription Base:** Pinpoint regional growth markets and high-yield demographic segments.
* **Maximize Engagement & Retention:** Map out peak consumption cycles and program performance leaderboards to eliminate subscriber churn.
* **Data-Driven Programming:** Transition scheduling and content acquisition from intuitive guesswork to hard metrics.

## 🔍 Data-Driven Discoveries & Core Metrics
Through extensive data cleaning and relational model building, the following system truths were uncovered across **4,386 unique validated subscribers**:

### 1. Geographic Footprint (Provincial Market Share)
Our regional analysis shows heavily centralized distribution, highlighting where marketing capital is winning and where expansion potential is untapped:
* **Gauteng (GP):** **36.6%** (`1,607` subscribers) — The undisputed anchor market.
* **Western Cape (WC):** **17.6%** (`770` subscribers) — Secondary core hub.
* **KwaZulu-Natal (KZN):** **10.2%** (`448` subscribers) — Critical baseline presence.
* **Other Regional Footprints:** Mpumalanga (**8.9%**), Limpopo (**7.8%**), Eastern Cape (**6.2%**), North West (**3.5%**), Free State (**3.4%**), and Northern Cape (**1.9%**).

### 2. Generational Audience Segmentation
The platform’s core engine is conclusively driven by mobile and upwardly mobile digital natives:
* **Young Adults (20–34):** **55.3%** (`2,424` subscribers) — The dominant user volume.
* **Mid-Age (35–49):** **33.6%** (`1,474` subscribers) — Stable, premium tier.
* **Teens (13–17):** **8.6%** (`377` subscribers) — Emerging target base.
* **Seniors (50+):** **2.5%** (`111` subscribers) — Niche consumption segment.

### 3. Multi-Variable Demographics (Gender & Race Intersections)
When cross-referencing gender identify against ethnic demographics, clear consumer blocks emerge:
* **Male-Dominant Segments:** Black males constitute the largest single consumption block at **32.2%** (`1,412` viewers), followed by Indian/Asian males at **16.1%** (`708`), and White males at **14.9%** (`653`).
* **Female-Dominant Segments:** Black females lead the female engagement profile at **5.7%** (`250` viewers).

## 🚀 Strategic Recommendations

### 📺 Content & Acquisition Alignment
* **Double-Down on Youth Programming:** Allocate **55%+** of the content acquisition budget toward local urban entertainment, lifestyle, and competitive live events catering directly to the 18–34 cohort.
* **Targeted Regional Campaigns:** Deploy hyper-localized marketing spend in Gauteng and Western Cape to lock down market dominance while setting up trial bundles for low-penetration provinces (e.g., Limpopo and Free State).

### 📈 Weekday Activation & Churn Mitigation
* **Combat Low Morning/Weekday Windows:** Introduce short-form, mobile-optimized "snackable" content formats (10–15 min summaries or daily mini-series) to capture engagement during early commuting and work lunch-breaks.
* **Gamification & Retainers:** Implement push-notification alerts tied to user-specific favorite genres exactly at peak evening windows (18:00 – 22:00) to optimize user retention and drive consistent viewing habits.
